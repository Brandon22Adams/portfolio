What is Ghostie?

Metroidvania games are some of my favorite games to play. I especially love Hollow Knight and Ori and the Blind Forest. When I saw there was going to be a Metroidvania game jam, I figured it would be a great chance to finally try making one myself. Since it was around Halloween, I decided to give it a spooky vibe with a ghost as the main character. I also wanted it to be kid-friendly so my nieces and nephews could play it. Plus, it was way easier to animate a floating ghost than a person.

The jam lasted 30 days, so I spent that month balancing work, life, and building a full game from concept to release. The game ended up placing 15th out of 87 entries in the Metroidvania category. After reading all the encouraging comments, I decided to keep going. I spent a few more months adding new assets, improving the lighting, and making the world feel more alive. I eventually released the full version on itch.io for free because I wanted as many people as possible to play it.

How I built Ghostie.

At this point I was feeling pretty comfortable with Unity and C#, so that became the core of the project. One of my big goals with this game was to push myself artistically. I spent a lot of time practicing and improving my drawing skills and ended up creating every asset and animation myself. It was honestly one of the most fun parts of the project.

What I learned.

This project showed me how important it is to have a real plan. With a deadline, I had to figure out when systems, levels, and characters needed to be done so I had enough time to test everything and get it ready for release. I also learned more about lighting, animation, and creating enemy AI. Overall, it helped me grow in both game development and art, and gave me a better understanding of how to manage a full project from start to finish.
