What is Sinta's Polymer Clayworks?

My spouse wanted to start her own polymer clay jewelry business and needed a website. Since I'm a professional web developer, I decided to build one from scratch and make it easy for her to add new products, remove old ones, and update the site whenever she wanted. She helped with the design, color schemes, and deciding what she wanted to show on each page. From there, I built everything from the frontend and backend to the Mongo database and deployment on DigitalOcean.

How I built Sinta's Polymer Clayworks.

I built the site using Angular for the frontend, Express.js for the backend, MongoDB for the database, and hosted everything through DigitalOcean. I integrated Stripe to handle payments and receipts, and I added OAuth so my wife and I could log in and manage products directly from the site.

I created a dynamic products page with filters based on whatever was in stock, along with filters for color, shape, and style. I also added sorting and search options to make browsing easier. I built a full cart system that connected to Stripe, handled payments, sent receipts, and notified us about what was ordered and where to ship everything.

What I learned.

This project taught me a lot about integration and working across the entire stack. Most of my professional career has been focused on frontend development, but this project pushed me to learn how backends connect to databases and how everything ties together on the hosting side. It helped me understand the full flow of data and what it actually takes to build a complete website from start to finish.
