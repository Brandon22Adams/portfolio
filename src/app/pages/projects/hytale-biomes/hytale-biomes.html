World design philosophy

My design process focuses on guiding exploration naturally. I like to create terrain shapes, points of interest, and environmental storytelling that reward curiosity and make players want to keep moving through an area instead of seeing everything at a glance. When I place structures, landmarks, caves, or encounters I usually start with questions like why is this here? Who built it? What role does it play in the environment around it?

Sunspire Plateaus

Sunspire Plateaus was my entry for the Hytale New Worlds Modding Contest. I created a biome that felt like it could exist naturally within Hytale's world but still had its own identity.

Sunspire Plateaus focuses heavily on exploration and environmental detail. Open plateaus transition into rivers, tropical islands, cave systems, and smaller handcrafted spaces designed to reward the players curiosity. A lot of the design process centered around making the world feel believable by thinking about why structures existed, how players would move through the terrain, and what would make someone want to stay in the area instead of leaving after a few minutes. To do that I created hidden encounters, glowing underground, buried treasure, biome specific crafting materials, and rare structures were designed to pique players interest.

The project includes over 100 custom prefabs, 26 custom items, new structures and encounters, unique weather, all tied directly into the biomes theme.

Technical Experience With Hytale World Gen v2

Most of my time with World Gen v2 has been focused on building terrain systems that feel natural to explore while still being purpose driven.

A lot of my technical work has gone into terrain masking, cave generation, and controlling where features appear within the world. One example of this is the cave systems in Sunspire Plateaus (see below). The caves are designed to generate specifically within cliff faces and plateau walls instead of appearing underwater or breaking through the terrain surface randomly. The goal was to make the generation feel natural and believable even though the systems behind it are fairly complex, and intentional.

I also put a lot of focus into gameplay flow when building terrain. I wanted to make traversing cliffs, rivers, caves, and elevation changes in survival fun without constantly forcing the player to dig or build around the terrain. A lot of the cave layouts and terrain transitions were created around ease of movement and exploration.

On the technical side I try to keep node trees organized, optimized, and maintainable. Systems are split into separate files, cached where possible to avoid unnecessary processing, and kept updated with newer node workflows instead of relying on deprecated systems. I enjoy the technical problem solving side of world generation just as much as the visual side.
